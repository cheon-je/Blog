Research

Research Interest: marriage, close relationships, interpersonal relationships, motivation, perception, sexuality, attachment, personality

I am broadly interested in the cognitive, motivational, and behavioral aspects of human interaction, particularly in understanding why people act the way they do and the potential consequences and trade-offs of their choices. My research primarily focuses on romantic relationships, examining the key predictors of relationship satisfaction and the factors that shape individuals’ relational experiences. To explore these questions, I integrate perspectives from regulatory focus theory, attachment, personality, and motivated perceptions, among others, to investigate how these psychological mechanisms contribute to both individual and relational well-being.

A significant part of my work involves dyadic analysis, utilizing assessments from both partners to examine how couples' perceptions influence marital satisfaction. One line of research has focused on the totality model, which evaluates the combined scores of both partners to predict overall well-being. My findings suggest that a couple’s combined neuroticism score is associated with lower marital satisfaction, highlighting the ways in which personality traits operate at the couple level to shape relationship dynamics.

In ongoing research, I am examining the complexities of attachment dynamics, specifically investigating the distinct mechanisms underlying anxious and avoidant attachment styles. This work seeks to clarify why these attachment orientations may lead to different relational and emotional outcomes, offering a more comprehensive understanding of how different forms of insecure attachment function in close relationships.

I am also exploring the role of sexuality in romantic relationships, particularly in the context of societal gender norms. My research examines whether traditional conceptions of masculinity and femininity, or broader constructs of agency and communion, have implications for the ways heterosexual dyads experience and navigate their sexual relationships.

Looking ahead, I aim to extend these lines of research beyond couples to examine how broader social structures and psychological processes shape interpersonal dynamics in diverse relational contexts. Through this work, I hope to contribute to a deeper understanding of how social and psychological factors interact to influence well-being in relationships and beyond.
