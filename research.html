Research

Research Interests: Attachment, Motivation, and Perception in Close Relationships; Dyadic Processes; Digital and AI-Mediated Interaction; Culture and Ecological Contexts

My research investigates the psychological architecture of human connection—how individuals regulate intimacy, interpret interpersonal signals, and negotiate motivational trade-offs across diverse relational and ecological contexts. Much of my work to date has focused on close and marital relationships, examining how individual differences in motivation, personality, and perception shape relational processes and satisfaction.

Building on this foundation, my recent and ongoing projects expand toward broader questions of connection both within and beyond romantic contexts. Working at the Barun ICT Research Center, my work increasingly centers on how individuals adapt to rapidly evolving technological ecologies—how they engage with artificial intelligence, construct social meaning in mediated environments, and adapt their strategies in response to technological shifts. This broader inquiry reflects my commitment to understanding the dynamic interplay between personal agency and shifting social ecologies in shaping broader human connection.

To investigate these dynamics, I employ a diverse range of quantitative methods, including longitudinal and dyadic modeling. My work draws on nationally representative panel datasets as well as custom-designed experimental paradigms and surveys, allowing me to examine psychological processes across levels and time.

Although grounded in theoretical frameworks, my research is also deeply informed by real-world concerns. With training in counseling psychology, I maintain a sustained interest in translating empirical findings into actionable insights and interventions that support relational resilience and psychological well-being. Whether through basic science or applied collaboration, my goal is to illuminate how individuals negotiate their emotional and social lives within a rapidly transforming interpersonal, cultural, and technological landscape.
